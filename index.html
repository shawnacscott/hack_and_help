Hack + Help

Hack + Help is a free, community-based user group for anyone in the Portland, Oregon area wanting to level up their technology skills. Whether you're new to coding, or just want to pick up a new language, framework, or tool, Hack + Help is the place to do it!

This group was born out of PDX.rb, but Hack + Help has now been rebooted as an independent, language agnostic group as of 2014. While we highly encourage peer-to-peer teaching, Hack + Help regularly has experienced attendees who are willing to help you out with Ruby, JavaScript, Python, HTML/CSS, and more. Bring your laptop and desire to learn and teach!

Participate:

  • Attend a meeting

    • General meetings

      • Come on out for a good old fashioned hackers meetup. Bring your projects and your problems and get ready to meet new people!

    • Learning cohorts

      • Want to learn a new skill with the accountability and support of a class, without all the lectures and cost? Learning a new skill in isolation is challenging, so if you have a topic you'd like to learn more about, come join us to learn together!

    • Presentation nights

      • On presentation nights, you can expect 20-30 minutes worth of lightning talks or interactive workshops. Participation is always optional, and we'll have space available for those who need to focus!

  • Give a presentation

    • Interested in presenting at Hack + Help? We would love to have you!

    Here's what to do:

    1. Post a message on our Google group letting us know what you'd like to present. We'll let you know when the next speaker's spot is available.
    2. Prepare a presentation on your topic of choice. This can be a quick lightning talk up to a 30-minute workshop. The more interactive and informative, the better!
    3. Let us know about any equipment (adapters, connectors, etc.) or supplies (pens, paper, etc.) you might need at least a week in advance.

  • Follow the Code of Conduct
